WHO: Disabilities

Disability is the result from an individual with a health condition and societal barriers that limit their ability to perform day-to-day tasks and participate fully in society.

Currently, about 16% of the global population are living with a significant disability. Around 70% of them are invisible and almost everyone will face a disability even if temporary once in their lives.

The percentage of people with significant disabilities due to different health conditions varies across the world regions, country income, individuals gender and other dimensions.

Europe and Americas are the regions with the highest percentage of people experiencing significant disabilities, while Africa has the lowest percentage.

When exclusively focusing on the severe disabilities prevalence, Americas becomes the first region in terms of affected people percentage and Africa's last position remains unchanged.

There is an extensive list of health conditions that may cause moderate and/or severe disabilities and the severity implied by each of them also varies.

For instance, Schizophrenia and African trypanosomiasis are solely associated with severe disabilities. And Low back pain, the main cause of disabilities worldwide, causes no more than moderate disabilities.

In contrast, most disabilities implied by Chronic kidney disease are severe. For each individual with moderate disabilities due to this condition, there can be other 25 people with severe disabilities caused by the same condition.

To come up with a summarized grasp of how these and other health conditions behave in different dimensions, they are grouped together by similar traits.

Musculoskeletal disorders imply reduced mobility and dexterity. It's the main group cause of significant disabilities worldwide, specially due to Low back pain.

Americas and Europe are the regions with the greater prevalence of significant disabilities caused by Musculoskeletal disorders.

Americas has also the unfortunate fact of being the region in which the prevalence of people with severe disabilities due to musculoskeletal conditions is 40% greater than the world average.

On the other hand, Africa is the only region where the group is not the leading, but the second, contributor to significant disabilities in its population.

Mental disorders entail significant disturbances in thinking, emotional regulation, or behavior. It's the second main cause of significant disabilities worldwide, specially due to Major depressive disorder and Anxiety disorders.

1 in every 3 people with a significant disability due to Major depressive disorder are experiencing severe impairments.

Again, Americas and Europe are the regions with the greater prevalence of significant disabilities caused by Mental Disorders.

Mental disorders is the main cause of significant disabilities in Africa.

Neurological disorders hit the central and peripheral nervous system and they constitute the third leading cause of significant disabilities. Migraine is the leading cause.

The prevalence of significant disabilities caused by Alzheimer's disease and dementias and Parkinson's disease are much greater in Europe, Western Pacific and Americas than in the rest of the world.

Africa has the lowest prevalence of all the causes in this group, but at Idiopathic epilepsy, in which it has the highest percentage of afflicted people. This is the only health condition in this group to imply severe disabilities.

There are 19 health condition group causes to significant disabilities, but Musculoskeletal, Mental, and Neurological disorders together correspond to about 60% of the causes to significant disabilities worldwide.

Now, let's have a glance in other interesting observations.

South-East Asia is the region with the highest percentage of people facing disabilities due to Vision loss disorders and diseases.

Ischemic stroke (Cardiovascular diseases) carries a high risk of death and the survivors can experience loss of vision and/or speech, paralysis and confusion. Disabilities caused by it has the highest prevalences at Western Pacific and Europe.

The turn over here is although Africa has the lowest prevalence of disabilities due to Ischemic stroke, its inhabitants suffers much more severe impairments than the ones in other regions. The proportion of severe disabilities caused by Ischemic stroke in Africa is almost twice as much the second region with the highest proportion of severe disabilities due to this condition.

WHO alerts Neonatal Preterm Birth is the leading cause of death in children under the age of 5 years. South-East Asia and Eastern Mediterranean has the highest percentage of people suffering for having been premature babies.

Western Pacific was the region with the lowest percentage of people with disabilities caused by COVID-19. The prevalence in there was around 92% lower than the world average.

African people still carry a large burden of disabilities due to HIV/AIDS and sexually transmitted infections and Neglected tropical diseases. The percentage of people with disabilities due to these condition groups in Africa is respectively 12x and 3.1x higher than the prevalence in Americas, the region with the second higher percentage of affected people by those groups.

Europe and Americas are the regions with the highest prevalence of severe disabilities due to Skin and subcutaneous diseases, while Africa is where people are the least affected by disabilities from this reason.

Americas has the leading percentage of people with moderate or severe disabilities due to Substance use disorders. The prevalence in this region is around 80% higher than the global average and the main reason is Opioids, a broad group of pain-relieving drugs that include heroin and morphine.
